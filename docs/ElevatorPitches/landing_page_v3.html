The Problem

Velocity only works when estimates mean something

Every roadmap assumes the underlying numbers are solid. But without a way to verify that, you're forecasting in the dark.

The translation problem

One team's "medium" is another team's "large." When you roll up estimates across squads, you're adding numbers that don't share a common language.

The drift problem

Even a well-calibrated team drifts over time. New hires, changing tech, shifting scope‚Äîbut nothing tells you when the baseline has moved.

The revision problem

When estimates change mid-flight, the original expectation vanishes. You can't learn from misses you never see.

The rollover problem

Incomplete work sneaks into the next sprint with a fresh estimate. Systemic optimism stays buried until the deadline arrives.

Fair questions, straight answers

You've seen tools promise to fix planning before. Here's where we're different‚Äîand where we're not.

"Measuring estimates creates bad incentives."
It can‚Äîif you measure the wrong thing. Tracking whether estimates are "right" encourages sandbagging and gaming. We track whether they're stable. Stable estimates can be calibrated and improved. Unstable ones are noise regardless of how accurate any single guess turns out to be.
"Teams will see this as surveillance."
That depends on how you use it‚Äîand we can't control that. What we can say: these indicators are designed as diagnostic instruments, not performance scorecards. A team with rising variance isn't failing; they have a signal that something shifted‚Äînew members, different problem space, process change‚Äîand an opportunity to recalibrate. If your culture would weaponise the data, address that first.
"We already have BI tools. Why add another?"
You could build this in eazyBI or Power BI. Most organisations don't, because it requires deep expertise and ongoing maintenance. We're not competing with your BI stack. We're providing a focused lens that would otherwise stay unbuilt‚Äîcontinuous visibility into whether the data feeding your existing reports is trustworthy.
"We're stretched thin. Another tool means more overhead."
Setup takes five minutes. No configuration wizards, no schema mapping, no training workshops. We read your existing Jira history and surface patterns immediately. If you have five minutes and a Jira Cloud instance, you can see your first estimation health snapshot today.
"What if the results are ugly?"
Then you've learned something important. Right now, you're making commitments backed by data of unknown quality. Ugly-but-known beats pretty-but-blind. If estimation health is poor, you can invest in improvement, dial back confidence in forecasts, or adopt different approaches for different teams. What you can no longer do is assume the numbers are solid when they're not.
